# Summary of Findings

## Dataset Overview
- The dataset contains **23,335 NBA games** from **2004–2020**.
- Seasons range from **2004 to 2020**.
- Home teams win **more than 50%** of games overall, confirming a home‑court advantage.

## Trends Over Time
- Home‑court advantage **declines after the 2013–2014 season**.
- Average scoring for both home and away teams **increases over time**.
- Three‑point shooting percentages for both home and away teams **rise steadily**, reflecting the league’s shift toward perimeter shooting.

## Three‑Point Shooting Insights (2018+)
- **25 team‑seasons** shot **≥ 37%** from three.
- Only **2** of those high‑accuracy teams still had losing records.
- **7 team‑seasons** shot **≤ 34%** and also had losing records.
- High 3‑point accuracy strongly correlates with winning.

## Key Conclusions
- **Higher 3‑point accuracy = higher win rates**, especially in modern seasons.
- **Low 3‑point accuracy is a disadvantage**, especially in a league increasingly driven by perimeter scoring.
- However, **3‑point shooting is not the only factor** in team success. Other important metrics include:
  - Elite defense  
  - Paint scoring and interior efficiency  
  - Ball movement and shot selection  
  - Free‑throw generation and physicality  

Overall, the analysis shows that while 3‑point shooting is a major driver of success, winning teams combine it with strong fundamentals across multiple areas.
